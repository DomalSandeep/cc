Practical 2: Creation of Virtual Machine

  1. Go to Home on Azure and click on “Virtual machines”
  2. Click on “Create” and select “Azure virtual machine - Create a virtual machine hosted by Azure”
  3. Enter the name of the virtual machine as well as change the region to India.
  4. Then click on “Review + create”. Once the validation is complete, click on “Create”
  5. The machine will be created along with the SSH keys. Download the private key pair present as a “PEM file” and save it. Click on “Go to resource”

  7. Follow the instructions given to connect the local machine i.e. copy the path of the private key (PEM File) and paste it. Then, open the local shell (Command Prompt) on the PC and execute the command.
  8. The command is executed as shown below in the Command Prompt and the local machine is now connected to the virtual machine.

Practical 8: AI Services in Azure

  1. Log in to Azure and click on “More Services”
  2. Under “AI + machine learning”, click on “Azure AI Services”
  3. Click on “Speech Service”
  4. Create a new Resource group and fill in the rest of the details. Under “Pricing tier”, click on “Free F0”
  5. Verify that all the details entered are correct and click “Create”
  6. Once the deployment is complete, click on “Go to resource”
  7. Under “Get Started”, click on “Go to Speech Studio”
  8. Speech studio is displayed below. Then click on “Captioning with speech to text”
  9. Under “Try it out”, click on “Try with your own video”
  10. Go to “YouTube” and copy the link of any video. Then go to “YouTube to MP3” and convert the video to MP4. Download it
  11. Navigate back to Speech Studio -> Captioning with speech to text. Upload the downloaded file
  12. The audio is converted from speech to text. You can change the mode, language, and the number of lines that you want to be displayed
  13. Under “Text to Speech”, click on “Voice Gallery”
  14. Under “Voice details”, click on “Go to Audio Content Creation”
  15. You can enter any text that will be converted into speech
  16. Under “Tuning”, select the three dots. Change the “Voice profile” to Tony and click Confirm
  17. Once you click on the “Play” button, the text will be converted to speech
  18. Go to “YouTube” and copy the link of any video. Then go to “YouTube to MP3” and convert the video to MP3. Download it
  19. Navigate to the “Speech Studio”. Under “Speech to Text”, select “Real-time speech to text”. Upload the audio file downloaded. The audio is now converted into text
  20. Go to “YouTube” and copy the link of any video. Then go to “YouTube to MP3” and convert the video to MP3. Download it
  21. Navigate to the “Speech Studio”. Under “Speech to Text”, select “Speech Translation”. Upload the audio file downloaded and change the target language to whichever language you want the text to be translated into. The audio is translated into the language you have chosen
  22. Log into Azure and click on “Azure AI Services”
  23. Under Azure AI Services, click on “Create Computer Vision”
  24. Add a new resource group and enter the details of the resource. Choose the pricing tier as “Free F0(20 Calls per minute, 5K Calls per month)
  25. Review the information entered and then click on “Create”
  26. Once the deployment is complete, click on “Go to Resource”
  27. Under “Get Started”, click on “Go to Vision Studio”
  28. The Vision Studio opens up on a new page. Here, click on “Add captions to images”
  29. Select the image that you want to add captions to or upload your own image
  30. Go back to the Vision Studio and now select “Extract common tags from images”. Here, you can drop your own file or use the pre-defined files and see the attributes that have been detected
  31. Go back to the Vision Studio and now select “Video Retrieval and Summary”. Click on “Industrial”. The video is then analyzed and summarized

Practical 10: Virtualization

  1. Login to Azure and click on “Virtual Machine”
  2. Create a virtual machine, namely VirtualMachine1
  3. Choose “Windows” from the list and then enter the details for the username and password (Remember the password used). Then select the checkbox and click “Review and Create”
  4. Click on “Create” and the virtual machine is deployed
  5. Once the deployment is complete, click on “Go to Resource”
  6. Click on “Connect” in VirtualMachine1
  7. In “Native RDP”, click on “Select”
  8. A pop-up window will appear and click on “Connect”
  9. Enter the credentials i.e. the username and password and then click “OK”
  10. Then click on Yes to proceed with the connection
  11. Follow the same steps and create a second virtual machine. Here, while creating the second virtual machine, go to the Networking tab and then make sure that the virtual network of the VM2 is the same as that of VM1
  12. Now, VirtualMachine1 will be open. Go to the Firewall Customize Settings and turn it off. Do the same for VirtualMachine2 as well
  13. In VirtualMachine2, go to the command prompt and type “ipconfig” to get the IPv4 address
  14. Go to VirtualMachine1 and ping VirtualMachine2 using the ping command and the IP address of VirtualMachine2
